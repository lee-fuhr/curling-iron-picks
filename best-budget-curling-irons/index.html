Curling irons don’t have to break the bank to deliver salon-worthy waves or bouncy curls. The challenge is finding a model that heats evenly, holds its temperature, and won’t fry your hair after months of use, especially when you’re on a tighter budget. When shopping on a budget, look for ceramic or tourmaline barrels (they distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz), adjustable temperature settings (around 200 to 400 degrees Fahrenheit lets you tailor heat to your hair type), and a reliable swivel cord that won’t twist mid-curl. You don’t need fancy extras; a solid clamp, comfortable handle, and automatic shut-off go a long way.
